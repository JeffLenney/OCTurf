The Anaheim Hills Engineering Challenge

Hillside Terrain + High-Heat Exposure = A System That Must Be Specified, Not Selected

Most turf contractors treat Anaheim Hills like a flat suburban yard. We don't. The hillside topography and 91-corridor heat profile require two distinct engineering disciplines working in tandem.

🏔️ Hillside Drainage & Retaining Wall Integration

Slope-graded lots require a drainage sub-base engineered to follow the grade -- not a flat perforated mat laid on a hillside. We calculate drainage flow rates based on your actual lot slope and integrate the turf system with existing retaining walls and hardscape. This prevents edge-lift, drainage backup, and turf shifting that plague hillside installs done without a proper site survey.

🌡️ 91 Corridor High-Heat Thermal Performance

Properties in the 91 corridor -- especially south- and west-facing yards -- can sustain ambient temperatures 8–12°F higher than coastal Orange County. Standard infill reaches 140–160°F on these lots. We specify our Cooling Infill evaporative mineral system, which reduces surface temperatures by up to 50°F using passive humidity recharge. The result: a lawn your family can use barefoot in July.

🏠 HOA Architectural Compliance

Anaheim Hills HOAs enforce strict color and pile-height restrictions. We maintain an approved palette of natural green blends that consistently pass architectural committee review. We submit color samples and specification sheets on your behalf before installation -- so your project doesn't get reversed after the fact.

💧 MWDOC Rebate Pre-Approval

Anaheim Public Utilities administers its own rebate program with different paperwork than standard MWDOC. We handle the entire application process: site documentation, product compliance submission, and post-installation inspection coordination. You receive the rebate check -- typically 30–60 days after final inspection.
