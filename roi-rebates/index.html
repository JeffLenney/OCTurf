2026 Rebate Strategy

The 2026 Hybrid Landscape Model: How the Rebate Funds the Entire Project

Most homeowners think the rebate covers turf. It doesn’t. Here’s the verified mechanic -- and why it’s actually more powerful than most people realize.

💡

The Technical Reality: MWDOC Rebates Grass Removal, Not the Turf

The MWDOC rebate program -- which covers Newport Beach, Irvine, Huntington Beach, and most other OC cities -- issues payment for the removal of existing irrigated grass, not for the turf product itself. The check is based on the square footage of grass you pull out, at $3.00 per square foot.

This distinction is critical, because it changes how the money flows through the project.

1
You remove existing grass from your yard. MWDOC pays you $3.00/sq ft for every square foot removed -- regardless of what replaces it.
2
You install a Hybrid Landscape: synthetic turf in the high-use zones (play areas, pet runs, patio surrounds) and California native drought-tolerant plants in the border and low-use areas.
3
The $3.00/sq ft rebate check arrives 30–60 days after the post-installation inspection -- and you use it to fund the entire project’s prep costs and native plant border installation. In many cases, the rebate covers all excavation, base material, grading, and border planting.
✅
The Hybrid Model converts the rebate from a discount into a full project funding mechanism. The turf pays for itself in water savings. The rebate pays for the prep and the native plant integration. Net out-of-pocket for the finished landscape is dramatically reduced.

The Anaheim Edge: $4.00/sq ft -- $1.00 More Than Every Other City

Anaheim Public Utilities customers are on a separate rebate program from the standard MWDOC pathway. Their rebate rate is $4.00 per square foot -- a full dollar more per square foot than the $3.00 rate available in Newport Beach, Irvine, and Huntington Beach.

On a 2,000 sq ft backyard, that’s the difference between $6,000 and $8,000 back. Maximum rebate in Anaheim: $20,000 on 5,000 sq ft. If your property is in Anaheim or Anaheim Hills, you are in the single best rebate jurisdiction in Orange County.
